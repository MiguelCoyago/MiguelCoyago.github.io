World of Warcraft

About World of Warcraft

World of Warcraft is a massively multiplayer online role-playing game (MMORPG) developed and published by Blizzard Entertainment. Released in November 2004, World of Warcraft was the first massively multiplayer game to achieve widespread popularity. As of December 2019, it is estimated that there are over 110 million active World of Warcraft accounts worldwide.

The game is set in the fictional world of Azeroth, a land of magic and adventure. Players assume the role of a character, customizing their appearance and abilities, and can explore the world, fight monsters, and complete quests.

World of Warcraft is a subscription-based game, and players must pay a monthly fee to play. The game is also available in a boxed retail version, which includes a free month of subscription time.

Features

  • Massively multiplayer: World of Warcraft is a massively multiplayer game, which means that there can be thousands of players online at the same time.
  • Open world: Players can explore the world of Azeroth freely, and there are no loading screens between zones.
  • Varied gameplay: There are many different activities that players can do in World of Warcraft, such as questing, exploring, fighting monsters, and crafting items.
  • Social aspect: World of Warcraft is a very social game, and players can interact with each other in a variety of ways, such as forming guilds, chatting, and trading items.

Expansions

World of Warcraft has been released in seven expansions since its initial release: The Burning Crusade, Wrath of the Lich King, Cataclysm, Mists of Pandaria, Warlords of Draenor, Legion, and Battle for Azeroth. Each expansion adds new content to the game, such as new zones, quests, and raids.

Reception

World of Warcraft has received critical acclaim since its release. It has won numerous awards, including Game of the Year from several publications. The game has also been a commercial success, with over 30 million copies sold worldwide.
